\hypertarget{simulation-set-up-and-overall-model-fitting-time}{%
\section{Simulation Set-Up and Overall Model Fitting
Time}\label{simulation-set-up-and-overall-model-fitting-time}}

When fitting each of our 4 models (\texttt{fullGPfixedL},
\texttt{NNGPblockFixedL}, \texttt{NNGPsequenFixedL}, and
\texttt{NNGPsequenVaryLj}) with \(m=8^2=64\) and \(T=500\), we specified
both \texttt{equalTimeDist\ =\ TRUE} and
\texttt{equalTimeDist\ =\ FALSE}. We thus have \(4\times 2 = 8\) model
settings.

Since \(m=64\) is quite small, we do not expect any significant
differences in the posterior sampling time between our 4 models with the
same \texttt{equalTimeDist} input. Since \(T=500\) is quite large, what
we have discussed in Appendix B of our manuscript suggest that the 3
temporal parameters' posterior sampling steps, especially the step for
\(\bm{\eta}_t\)'s, can be markedly accelerated by adopting our
approaches and specifying \texttt{equalTimeDist\ =\ TRUE} for each of
our 4 models.

The results we have obtained corroborate these well. With
\texttt{equalTimeDist\ =\ TRUE} specified, the overall model fitting
time for our 4 models, i.e., \texttt{fullGPfixedL},
\texttt{NNGPblockFixedL}, \texttt{NNGPsequenFixedL}, and
\texttt{NNGPsequenVaryLj}, are 7.04 hours, 7.03 hours, 7.04 hours, and
6.31 hours, respectively.\footnote{It is worth noting that we utilized a
  version of our package with the extra step-wise time recording code
  portions to fit the above models. This package version is slower than
  our original package version presented in the GitHub repository.} If
we do not take advantage of our tactics for evenly dispersed time points
presented in Appendix B of our manuscript by specifying
\texttt{equalTimeDist\ =\ FALSE} instead, we will need 28.98 days, 29.09
days, 29.03 days, and 28.74 days to fit the same 4 models using the same
computation resources.
